WAN Technologies Beyond CCNA

Some WAN technologies are no longer taught in detail at CCNA level. Instead of configuration, CCNA focuses on awareness. Below is a simple conceptual explanation of these technologies.

PPP — Point-to-Point Protocol

What it is: A protocol used to directly connect two routers over a WAN link.

Why it exists: To transport network traffic over serial connections with optional authentication.

Where you see it: Older leased lines and legacy WAN infrastructures.

GRE — Generic Routing Encapsulation

What it is: A tunneling protocol that wraps packets inside another packet.

Why it exists: To connect distant networks as if they were directly linked.

Where you see it: Site-to-site tunnels, often combined with IPsec.

BGP — Border Gateway Protocol

What it is: The routing protocol used to exchange routes between organizations.

Why it exists: To control how traffic flows across the Internet.

Where you see it: ISPs, data centers, large enterprise networks.

CCNA focuses on awareness only — detailed configuration is covered in CCNP and higher levels.
