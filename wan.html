WAN and How the Internet Works

1. WAN (Wide Area Network)

A WAN is a network that covers a large geographic area, connecting multiple smaller networks like LANs. It allows data to travel over long distances using technologies like fiber optic cables, satellites, and leased lines.

2. How the Internet Works

The internet is a global network of networks. Data travels in packets through routers and switches across cables, fiber optics, and undersea cables. ISPs provide the connection for homes and businesses, while IANA and RIRs manage IP addresses globally.

3. APNIC

APNIC (Asia Pacific Network Information Centre) is the Regional Internet Registry (RIR) for the Asia-Pacific region. It manages IP addresses and AS numbers for ISPs and organizations in this region.

4. ISP (Internet Service Provider)

ISPs provide internet access to users by connecting them to larger networks. They use infrastructure like fiber, DSL, and mobile networks, and they get IP addresses and routing information from RIRs like APNIC.

5. Sea Route Lines Connected to India

India is connected to multiple submarine fiber optic cables, including:

  • SEA-ME-WE 3 & 4
  • India-Middle East-Western Europe (IMEWE)
  • Bay of Bengal Gateway (BBG)
Major landing points in India: Mumbai, Chennai, Cochin, and Kochi.

6. RIR (Regional Internet Registry)

RIRs manage the allocation of IP addresses and AS numbers in their respective regions. Example: APNIC for Asia-Pacific, RIPE NCC for Europe, ARIN for North America.

7. IANA (Internet Assigned Numbers Authority)

IANA is responsible for globally coordinating IP addresses, domain names, and protocol numbers. It delegates blocks of IPs to RIRs, who then distribute them regionally.

8. Edge Devices

Edge devices are hardware that connects end-users to the internet, such as routers, switches, modems, and IoT devices. They handle local network traffic and forward data to the wider internet.
