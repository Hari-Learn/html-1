Segment vs Fragment

Understanding segmentation and fragmentation for the CCNA exam

High-Level Overview

In networking, data is broken into smaller pieces to travel efficiently across a network. Two commonly confused terms in CCNA are Segment and Fragment.

Although both involve breaking data into smaller units, they occur at different OSI layers and for different reasons.

What is a Segment?

A segment is a unit of data created at the Transport Layer (Layer 4).

When applications send data, TCP or UDP breaks the data into manageable pieces called segments before passing them down the OSI stack.

Key Points About Segments

  • Created by TCP or UDP
  • Occurs at Layer 4
  • Includes port numbers
  • TCP segments provide reliability

What is a Fragment?

A fragment is created at the Network Layer (Layer 3).

Fragmentation happens when an IP packet is larger than the MTU (Maximum Transmission Unit) of the outgoing link.

Key Points About Fragments

  • Occurs at Layer 3
  • Handled by IP
  • Related to MTU size
  • Fragments are reassembled at destination
