IPv6 vs IPv4 Subnetting

Explained Like You're 10 🎒 (CCNA Style)

Start Learning 🚀

🌍 Think of IP Addresses Like Houses

IP addresses are like home addresses for computers. Subnetting means dividing a big city into smaller neighborhoods.

🏠 IPv4 – Small City

  • • 32 bits
  • • Limited addresses 😬
  • • Lots of subnet math 🧮
  • • Uses broadcast

🌎 IPv6 – Giant Planet

  • • 128 bits 🤯
  • • Almost unlimited addresses
  • • Usually just use /64
  • • No broadcast (multicast instead)

🧠 ELI10 Super Simple Difference

IPv4 is like trying to fit everyone into a small town. You must carefully divide space and count houses.


IPv6 is like having an entire planet. You don’t worry about running out of space. Subnetting is easier because most networks just use /64 and move on.

🎓 CCNA Exam Tip

✔ Remember: IPv6 standard subnet = /64

✔ IPv6 does NOT use broadcast

✔ IPv6 has way more addresses than IPv4

✔ IPv6 uses hexadecimal format
