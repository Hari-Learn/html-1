Optical Module – Complete Guide (CCNA Level)

What is an Optical Module?

An optical module (SFP, SFP+, QSFP) is a pluggable transceiver used to convert electrical signals into light signals for fiber optic communication.

  • Used for fiber networking
  • Supports high speed transmission
  • Pluggable & replaceable

Why Does It Exist?

  • Copper cable distance limitation (100m)
  • Need higher bandwidth
  • Reduce interference
  • Long-distance connectivity

When Do We Need It?

  • Connecting buildings
  • Data center connections
  • ISP backbone links
  • Distance greater than 100m

Works in Switch or Router?

Optical modules work in:

  • Layer 2 Switch
  • Layer 3 Switch
  • Router

The device must have an SFP/SFP+ slot.

  • SFP – 1 Gbps
  • SFP+ – 10 Gbps
  • QSFP – 40 Gbps
  • QSFP28 – 100 Gbps
