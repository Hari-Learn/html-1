Threat & Incident Glossary

Short, practical definitions for common security concepts — arranged as small inline tiles so you can scan them quickly.

Vulnerability

A weakness in software, hardware, or procedures that can be exploited to cause unintended behavior or loss. Examples: unpatched software, misconfigured permissions, insecure defaults.

Weakness → Exploit

Threat

Anything that can potentially cause harm to an asset — usually an actor (e.g., hacker, insider) or event (e.g., natural disaster) capable of exploiting vulnerabilities.

Actor / Event

Risk

The likelihood that a threat will exploit a vulnerability and the impact if it does. Risk = Likelihood × Impact. Risk drives prioritization and mitigation choices.

Likelihood × Impact

Payload

The harmful component delivered by an exploit — e.g., a malicious script, ransomware binary, or data exfiltration routine. Payload does the “damage” after access is gained.

Exploit output

Vigilance

Continuous awareness and monitoring to detect anomalies and suspicious behavior early. Includes logging, alerts, routine audits, threat hunting, and employee awareness.

Monitor & Respond

Autopsy (Post‑mortem)

A structured investigation after an incident to understand root cause, timeline, and missed controls. Goal: learn, document, and prevent recurrence with concrete action items.

Root cause analysis

OSINF (OSINT)

Open‑Source Intelligence: collecting publicly available information (web, social, registries) to profile targets, discover attack surface, or gather context for investigations.

Public data

Putting it Together

Example flow: OSINT reveals exposed asset → vulnerability exists → threat actor crafts payload → likelihood + impact define risk → vigilance detects activity → autopsy improves defenses.

Practical Tips

Prioritize fixes by risk, monitor high‑value assets, keep inventories up to date, practice incident response drills, and document autopsies with measurable follow-ups.

Short Checklist

Inventory → Patch → Harden → Monitor → Back up → Test response → Perform autopsies and close gaps.
