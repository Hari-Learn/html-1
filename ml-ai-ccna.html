CCNA 2026 – AI & ML (ELI10)

What you need to learn • What Cisco will ask • What engineers should understand

First… Do I Need to Be a Data Scientist?

❌ No. CCNA does NOT expect coding models or math.

CCNA expects you to understand how AI helps networks, not how to build AI.

Artificial Intelligence (AI)

ELI10: AI is when a system can think, decide, and act like a human.

In networking, AI helps systems make smart decisions automatically.

CCNA expects you to know:

  • AI helps reduce manual work
  • AI improves network visibility
  • AI assists troubleshooting
Engineer mindset:
AI supports engineers — it does NOT replace them.

Machine Learning (ML)

ELI10: ML is how computers learn from past data.

Example: If the network was slow every Monday, ML remembers and predicts it.

CCNA expects you to know:

  • ML finds patterns in network data
  • ML predicts problems before they happen
  • ML improves monitoring tools
Engineer mindset:
Garbage data = bad ML results.

Generative & Predictive AI

Generative AI:
Creates things (alerts, suggestions, configs).

Predictive AI:
Tells you what might happen next.

CCNA expects you to know:

  • Predict link failures or congestion
  • Generate troubleshooting insights
  • Assist in faster decision-making
Engineer mindset:
AI gives suggestions — humans approve changes.

What Will Be Asked in CCNA 2026?

  • Basic definition of AI and ML
  • How AI/ML helps network operations
  • Predictive vs Generative AI (concept only)
  • Why AI-driven monitoring is useful
  • Human vs AI decision responsibility

One-Line Memory Trick

AI = smart decisions
ML = learning from data
Predictive AI = future warning
Generative AI = smart suggestions

CCNA checks understanding, not implementation.
