Rate Limiting

Controlling request traffic to protect systems

What is Rate Limiting?

Rate limiting is a technique used in APIs and distributed systems to limit the number of requests a client can make within a specific time window.

Why It Is Important

  • Prevents server overload
  • Protects against abuse or DDoS attacks
  • Ensures fair usage among clients
  • Improves system stability

Common Algorithms

Token Bucket

Allows bursts while controlling long-term rate.

Leaky Bucket

Processes requests at a constant rate.

Fixed Window

Counts requests in fixed time intervals.
