Sidecar Pattern

📌 What is it?

The Sidecar Pattern attaches auxiliary services (like logging, monitoring, or security) alongside a main microservice to extend its functionality without modifying it.

🚀 Why use it?

  • Separation of concerns
  • Reusable supporting services
  • Easier maintenance

⚠ Challenges

  • Increased resource usage
  • Deployment complexity
  • Communication overhead

🧒 Explain Like I'm 10

Imagine riding a bike 🚲 with a sidecar:

  • The bike is your main service
  • The sidecar carries extra stuff (tools, luggage) 🧳
  • The bike doesn’t change — but gains extra capabilities

👉 That extra helper attached to the service is called a Sidecar.

How It Works

  1. Main service handles business logic
  2. Sidecar runs alongside in the same environment
  3. Sidecar intercepts or enhances communication
  4. Provides features like logging, monitoring, or retries
