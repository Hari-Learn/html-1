Database Sharding

Explained simply (ELI10)

❓ Why do we need Sharding?

Imagine you have one huge cupboard storing all your books 📚.

As more books come in, it becomes:

  • Too slow to find anything 😵
  • Too full to store more 😬
  • Easy to break under pressure 💥

So instead of one giant cupboard, you split books into multiple cupboards.

📦 What is Sharding?

Sharding means splitting your data into smaller pieces and storing them in different databases (called shards).

Shard 1
Shard 2
Shard 3

Each shard stores only a portion of the total data.

⚙️ How does Sharding work?

Data is split using different strategies:

Range-Based

Users 1–1000 → Shard 1

Hash-Based

User ID → Hash → Shard

Directory-Based

Lookup table decides shard
