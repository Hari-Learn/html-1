RAG Series • Part 3

What is Chunking?

Learn how RAG breaks large documents into smaller pieces so AI can search and understand information better.

Start Learning

Imagine Reading a Huge Book 📚

Imagine your teacher gives you a 1000-page book and asks:

“Find the rules about school exams.”

Reading the entire book every single time would be super slow.

So instead, we divide the book into smaller sections .

Those small pieces are called Chunks .

Simple Definition

Chunking means:

Breaking Big Text → Into Small Pieces

Why Do We Need Chunking?

🐌

Large Documents Are Slow

AI cannot efficiently search giant files every time.

🧠

AI Has Context Limits

AI models cannot read unlimited text at once.

🎯

Better Search Accuracy

Smaller chunks help retrieve exactly the needed information.

Simple Chunking Example

Original Document:

“The library opens at 8AM. Students must carry ID cards. Mobile phones are not allowed during exams. Late submissions lose marks.”

After Chunking:

Chunk 1

“The library opens at 8AM.”

Chunk 2

“Students must carry ID cards.”

Chunk 3

“Mobile phones are not allowed during exams.”

Chunk 4

“Late submissions lose marks.”

Chunking Strategies in RAG

📏

1. Fixed Size Chunking

Split text into equal sizes.

500 words
500 words
500 words
500 words

✅ Good

Simple and fast.

❌ Bad

Can break sentences in weird places.

📝

2. Sentence Chunking

Split text sentence by sentence.

Sentence 1
Sentence 2
Sentence 3

Better readability and cleaner meaning.

📄

3. Paragraph Chunking

Keep full paragraphs together.

Paragraph 1
Paragraph 2

Great for preserving context and meaning.

🔗

4. Overlapping Chunking

Some text is repeated between chunks.

Chunk 1 → “AI helps students learn faster.”
Chunk 2 → “students learn faster and improve skills.”

✅ Good

Prevents losing important context.

⚠️ Tradeoff

Uses more storage space.

🧠

5. Semantic Chunking

Split based on meaning instead of size.

All text related to “exam rules” stays together in one chunk, even if it is long.

This is smarter and often gives better RAG results.

Which Strategy is Best?

⚡

Fast Projects

Use fixed-size chunking.

📖

Better Readability

Use sentence or paragraph chunking.

🚀

Advanced AI Systems

Use semantic + overlapping chunking.

Final Understanding

Chunking is simply:

Divide Large Knowledge → Into Searchable Pieces

Good chunking = better retrieval, better context, and better AI answers.
