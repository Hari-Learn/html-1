RAG Retrieval

A simple ELI10 explanation of what Retrieval means in RAG, why it is important, and how AI uses it to find the right information.

What is Retrieval?

Imagine your teacher asks:

“What is the capital of Japan?”

Before answering, you first search your school book and find the correct information:

“Tokyo is the capital of Japan.”

That searching step is called Retrieval.

In RAG systems, Retrieval means:

AI searches documents, PDFs, websites, or databases to find useful information before generating an answer.

Why is Retrieval Important?

📚

Finds Correct Information

Instead of guessing, AI first searches trusted data sources for answers.

🧠

Makes AI Smarter

Retrieval gives extra knowledge to the AI model, helping it answer better.

⚡

Uses Latest Data

AI can retrieve new documents and updated information without retraining the whole model.

How Retrieval Works in RAG

1

Store Knowledge

Documents, PDFs, websites, and notes are stored inside a database.

2

User Asks a Question

Example: “What are the benefits of solar energy?”

3

Retrieval Searches Data

The system searches for the most relevant content related to the question.

4

Best Information is Selected

The retrieval system picks the most useful text chunks.

5

AI Generates Final Answer

The retrieved information is sent to the AI model to create the final response.

Easy Example

User Question

“What are planets made of?”

Retrieval Finds

“Planets are made of rock, gas, ice, and metals depending on the planet type.”

Retrieval Flow

User Question
➡️
Search Documents
➡️
Find Best Match
➡️
Send to AI
➡️
Final Answer
