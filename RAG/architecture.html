Main Parts of RAG Architecture

📚

1. Documents

These are the knowledge sources.

PDFs, websites, notes, Word files, company documents, or databases.

Example: School rulebook PDF

✂️

2. Chunking

Big documents are too large for AI to search easily.

So we cut documents into small pieces called chunks.

Chunk 1
Chunk 2
Chunk 3
🧠

3. Embeddings

Computers do not truly understand words like humans.

So text is converted into numbers called vectors or embeddings . 

"Library closes at 5PM"

↓

[0.12, 0.87, 0.44, 0.91 ...]
🗄️

4. Vector Database

All embeddings are stored in a special database.

This database helps find similar information very fast.

Think of it like a super smart library shelf.

🔎

5. Retriever

When a user asks a question, the retriever searches the vector database.

It finds the most relevant chunks.

“Find chunks related to library rules.”

🤖

6. LLM (AI Model)

Finally, the retrieved chunks are sent to the AI model.

The AI reads the information and generates a human-friendly answer.

Why This Architecture is Powerful

⚡

Fast Search

Finds useful information quickly from huge amounts of data.

🎯

Better Accuracy

AI answers using real documents instead of random guessing.

🔒

Private Knowledge

Companies can use their own internal data safely.

Final Simple Understanding

RAG Architecture is basically:

Store Knowledge → Find Relevant Data → Generate Smart Answer
