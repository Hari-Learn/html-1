RAG Chunking

Understanding Fixed Size Chunking in simple "Explain Like I'm 10" style 🚀

What is RAG?

Imagine you have a super smart robot friend 🤖.

But the robot cannot remember every book in the world. So before answering your question, it quickly looks inside notes and books to find useful information.

That is basically what RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) does.

What is Chunking?

Think About a Huge Book 📚

If the robot tries to read the whole book at once, it becomes slow and confused.

So We Split It ✂️

We cut the big book into smaller pieces called chunks.

Fixed Size Chunking

The easiest and most common chunking method

What Is It?

Fixed size chunking means:

"Cut text into equal-sized pieces."

Example:

Chunk 1

First 100 words

Chunk 2

Next 100 words

Chunk 3

Another 100 words

Why Do We Use Fixed Size Chunking?

⚡

Faster

Small pieces are quicker for the AI to search through.

🧠

Easier to Understand

The AI focuses on one small idea at a time.

💰

Saves Money

Smaller chunks use fewer AI tokens and less computing power.

One Small Problem 😅

Sometimes the text gets cut in weird places.

Example:

Chunk 1: "The dinosaur lived in the..."

Chunk 2: "...forest and ate plants."

The meaning can break because the sentence was split in half.

Quick Summary 🎯

  • ✅ RAG helps AI search information before answering.
  • ✅ Chunking means splitting large text into smaller pieces.
  • ✅ Fixed size chunking cuts text into equal parts.
  • ✅ It is simple, fast, and cheap.
  • ⚠️ But sometimes it cuts sentences awkwardly.
