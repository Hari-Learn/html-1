STP VS RSTP

STP (Spanning Tree Protocol)

  • • IEEE 802.1D standard
  • • Slow convergence (30–50 seconds)
  • • Uses blocking, listening, learning states
  • • Suitable for small networks
  • • No rapid failover

RSTP (Rapid Spanning Tree Protocol)

  • • IEEE 802.1w standard
  • • Fast convergence (1–6 seconds)
  • • Uses discarding, learning, forwarding states
  • • Ideal for modern & large networks
  • • Rapid failover support
