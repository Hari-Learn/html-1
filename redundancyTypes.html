Network Redundancy (Explained Like You're 10)

Redundancy means having a backup plan. If one thing breaks, another one is ready to help. Just like having an extra pencil in your bag!

🔗 Link Redundancy

Imagine two bridges connecting the same two cities. If one bridge is closed, cars can use the other bridge.

In networks, this means having two cables (or connections) between devices. If one cable breaks, data uses the other one.

💻 Device Redundancy

Think of having two teachers in a classroom. If one is sick, the other can still teach.

In networking, this means having two routers or switches. If one device stops working, the backup device takes over.

🛣️ Path Redundancy

Imagine going to school. You can take the main road or a side street.

If there’s traffic on one road, you take another. In networks, data can travel through different routes to reach its destination.

🔌 Power Redundancy

Imagine your house has a generator. If electricity goes out, the generator keeps things running.

In networks, important devices have two power supplies. If one power source fails, the other keeps the system alive.
