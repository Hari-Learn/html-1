Understanding Switch Stacking Concepts

What is Switch Stacking?

Switch stacking is a technology that allows multiple physical switches to operate as a single logical switch. This simplifies management, improves redundancy, and increases scalability in enterprise networks.

How Switch Stacking Works

  • Switches are connected using special stacking cables or stack ports.
  • One switch becomes the Master, others act as Members.
  • The stack shares a single control plane and management IP address.
  • Configuration is done on the master switch and applied to the entire stack.

Advantages of Switch Stacking

  • Simplified management (single IP address)
  • High availability and redundancy
  • Increased port density
  • Better scalability

Switch Stacking vs Standalone Switches

Feature Switch Stacking Standalone Switch
Management Single IP Multiple IPs
Redundancy High Limited
Scalability Easy expansion Manual configuration
