Switch Stacking

CCNA Concept Made Simple

What is Switch Stacking?

Switch stacking lets multiple switches act like **one big switch**.

Think of it like a **team with one leader**—you manage everything from one place.

How It Works

Switch 1 (Master) 🔗 Switch 2 🔗 Switch 3

All switches connect using stacking cables and behave as one system.

⚙️

Key Behavior

  • • One Master switch controls the stack
  • • Other switches act as members
  • • Shared configuration across all switches
  • • Single management IP address
🚀

Advantages

  • • Easier management (one device view)
  • • High availability (failover support)
  • • More ports (combined switches)
  • • Easy to scale (add more switches)
🧠

ELI10 Analogy

Imagine a classroom where one teacher (master) controls multiple groups of students (switches). Even though there are many groups, everything is managed from one place.

💻

Multiple switches → One master → Single control → Easy management
