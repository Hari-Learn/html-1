Clean Architecture Pattern

Clean Architecture organizes software into layers with clear boundaries, ensuring that business rules remain independent of frameworks, databases, user interfaces, and external systems.

Overview

Proposed by Robert C. Martin (Uncle Bob), Clean Architecture emphasizes separation of concerns and dependency management. Dependencies always point inward toward the core business rules, making the application easier to maintain, test, and evolve.

Core Layers

Entities

Enterprise-wide business rules and core domain models.

Use Cases

Application-specific business rules and workflows.

Interface Adapters

Controllers, presenters, gateways, and data transformation logic.

Frameworks & Drivers

Databases, web frameworks, UI frameworks, and external services.

All dependencies point inward toward the business core.

Example: Online Shopping Application

  • Entities: Product, Customer, Order.
  • Use Cases: Place Order, Cancel Order, Process Payment.
  • Interface Adapters: REST Controllers, DTOs, Repositories.
  • Frameworks & Drivers: Spring Boot, PostgreSQL, Payment Gateway.

Benefits

  • Strong separation of concerns.
  • Highly testable business logic.
  • Framework-independent architecture.
  • Easy to replace databases and external services.
  • Well-suited for large and long-lived applications.

Challenges

  • Requires more initial design effort.
  • Can introduce many interfaces and abstractions.
  • May feel complex for small CRUD applications.
  • Developers must understand dependency inversion principles.
