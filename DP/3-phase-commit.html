1

CanCommit?

  • • Coordinator asks if participants can commit.
  • • Participants validate the transaction.
  • • Respond with YES or NO.
2

PreCommit

  • • Coordinator sends PreCommit.
  • • Participants prepare to commit.
  • • Transaction is ready but not yet finalized.
3

DoCommit

  • • Coordinator sends the final commit.
  • • All participants permanently commit.
  • • If timeout occurs, recovery is easier than 2PC.

Two-Phase Commit (2PC)

  • • 2 phases
  • • Blocking protocol
  • • Coordinator failure can delay recovery

Three-Phase Commit (3PC)

  • • 3 phases
  • • Adds a PreCommit stage
  • • Reduces blocking using timeouts and better recovery

Key Idea

Three-Phase Commit (3PC) extends Two-Phase Commit by introducing an intermediate PreCommit phase. This extra step helps participants recover more safely from coordinator failures and reduces the chances of blocking in distributed transaction systems. Footer will be added.
