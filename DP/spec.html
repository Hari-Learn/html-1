Specification Design Pattern

Encapsulating business rules into reusable and composable specifications.

What is the Specification Pattern?

The Specification Pattern allows business rules and filtering logic to be represented as reusable objects called specifications. These specifications can be combined using logical operators such as AND, OR, and NOT to create complex business conditions without duplicating code.

How It Works

Entity

Customer / Product

→
Specification

Business Rule

→
Result

Match or Reject

Example Scenario

Suppose an e-commerce application needs to find products that:

  • Are in stock
  • Cost less than $100
  • Belong to the Electronics category

Each rule becomes a separate specification and can be combined:

InStockSpecification
AND PriceLessThanSpecification
AND ElectronicsCategorySpecification

Advantages

  • Reusable business rules
  • Improves code readability
  • Easy composition of complex queries
  • Supports separation of concerns

Challenges

  • Can introduce extra abstraction
  • More classes to maintain
  • May be excessive for simple rules
  • Requires careful design for complex systems

Specification Composition

AND

All conditions must be true.

OR

At least one condition must be true.

NOT

Negates a condition.

The Specification Pattern helps build flexible, reusable, and maintainable business rule logic.
