Anti-Corruption Layer (ACL)

The Anti-Corruption Layer is a Domain-Driven Design (DDD) pattern that protects a modern application from the complexities and inconsistencies of external or legacy systems.

Think of the ACL as a translator. It converts data and requests between your application's domain model and an external system's model, preventing external concepts from leaking into your codebase.

Why Use It?

  • Protects your domain model from legacy systems.
  • Reduces coupling between systems.
  • Makes future system replacements easier.
  • Improves maintainability and flexibility.

How It Works

  1. Your application sends a request to the ACL.
  2. The ACL translates the request format.
  3. The external system processes the request.
  4. The ACL converts the response back to your domain model.
  5. Your application works only with its own business concepts.

Example

A modern e-commerce application integrates with a legacy ERP system. The ERP uses fields like CUST_ID and ORD_AMT, while the application uses CustomerId and OrderTotal. The Anti-Corruption Layer handles the mapping between them.
