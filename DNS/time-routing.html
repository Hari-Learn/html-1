DNS Routing Policy

Time Window Routing

Time Window Routing directs traffic to different servers or endpoints based on a specific time schedule, date range, or business hours.

How It Works

⏰

Define Schedule

Configure routing rules for specific times, dates, weekdays, or business hours.

🔀

Traffic Switching

DNS responses change automatically based on the active time window.

🌍

Automated Routing

Users are routed to different endpoints during peak hours, maintenance windows, or events.

Example Scenario 

08:00 AM - 06:00 PM
→ Route traffic to Primary Production Server

06:00 PM - 08:00 AM
→ Route traffic to Backup / Low-Cost Server

Weekend
→ Route traffic to Maintenance Environment

Benefits

  • ✅ Automates scheduled routing
  • ✅ Useful for maintenance windows
  • ✅ Reduces infrastructure costs
  • ✅ Supports business-hour traffic patterns
  • ✅ Helps with disaster recovery planning

Limitations

  • ❌ Not suitable for real-time load balancing
  • ❌ DNS cache delays may affect switching
  • ❌ Requires careful schedule planning
  • ❌ Time zone handling can be complex
  • ❌ Less dynamic than latency-based routing

Common Use Cases

🛠️

Maintenance Windows

Redirect traffic during planned upgrades.

💰

Cost Optimization

Use cheaper infrastructure during off-hours.

📈

Peak Traffic Handling

Scale routing during business hours.

🧪

Scheduled Testing

Route users to testing environments temporarily.
