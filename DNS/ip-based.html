IP-Based / CIDR-Based Routing

Route DNS traffic based on the client’s source IP address or CIDR block instead of geographic location or latency.

What is IP-Based Routing?

IP-based routing (or CIDR-based routing) is a DNS traffic management policy where users are directed to different endpoints depending on their source IP range. CIDR blocks allow administrators to define routing behavior for specific networks, ISPs, corporate offices, regions, or cloud providers.

🌍

Example Scenario

Users from the CIDR range 203.0.113.0/24 are routed to the Asia data center, while users from 198.51.100.0/24 are routed to the Europe region.

⚡

Why Use It?

  • ✔ ISP-specific traffic steering
  • ✔ Enterprise network segmentation
  • ✔ Compliance & regional restrictions
  • ✔ Cloud provider optimization
  • ✔ Custom traffic engineering

Sample CIDR Routing Rules

CIDR Block Region Target Endpoint
203.0.113.0/24 Asia asia.example.com
198.51.100.0/24 Europe eu.example.com
192.0.2.0/24 US East us-east.example.com

Key Benefits

Better Control

Administrators gain precise control over how specific networks are routed across infrastructure.

Improved Performance

Traffic can be directed toward optimized or preferred endpoints for certain ISPs and enterprise networks.

Compliance

Organizations can enforce region-specific routing rules for legal or business requirements.
