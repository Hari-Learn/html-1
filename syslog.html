Syslog Severity Levels

Memorize the Order! (Often tested in drag-and-drop)

Explain Like I'm 10 👶

Imagine your network devices (like routers and switches) are kids in a classroom. When something happens, they raise their hand and tell the teacher.

But not all problems are the same. Some are VERY serious 🚨 and some are just small updates 🙂

Syslog severity levels tell us how serious the message is.

Severity Levels (0 = Most Serious)

Level Name ELI10 Meaning
0 Emergency Everything is broken! 🔥 The whole network is down!
1 Alert Fix this NOW! 🚨 Serious problem happening.
2 Critical Big problem. Important service stopped.
3 Error Something failed, but not everything.
4 Warning Be careful! ⚠ Something might become a problem.
5 Notification Just letting you know something changed.
6 Informational Normal updates. Everything is okay 🙂
7 Debug Super detailed technical messages for engineers.

Why Is This Important? 🤔

  • Helps network engineers know how serious a problem is.
  • Prevents small issues from becoming big disasters.
  • Used in monitoring systems and SIEM tools.
  • Very common in CCNA exam questions.

When Is It Used? 📅

Every time a router, switch, or firewall sends a message to a Syslog server.

Example: If an interface goes down, you might see Level 3 (Error). If the whole system crashes, you might see Level 0 (Emergency).
