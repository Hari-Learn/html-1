Optical Fibre vs Copper vs Wireless vs Radio Waves

Comparison of Transmission Media

Optical Fibre

Includes: Glass or plastic fibers transmitting light

Features: Very high bandwidth, low signal loss

Advantages:

  • Extremely fast data transfer
  • Immune to electromagnetic interference
  • Long-distance communication

Disadvantages:

  • High installation cost
  • Fragile compared to copper

Examples: Internet backbone, FTTH

Copper

Includes: Twisted pair, coaxial cables

Features: Electrical signal transmission

Advantages:

  • Low cost
  • Easy to install and maintain

Disadvantages:

  • Limited bandwidth
  • Susceptible to interference

Examples: Ethernet cables, telephone lines

Wireless

Includes: Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, Infrared

Features: No physical cables

Advantages:

  • Mobility and flexibility
  • Quick deployment

Disadvantages:

  • Security concerns
  • Limited range and speed

Examples: Wi-Fi networks, wireless keyboards

Radio Waves

Includes: AM/FM, cellular, satellite

Features: Long-range electromagnetic waves

Advantages:

  • Wide coverage area
  • Supports mobility

Disadvantages:

  • Prone to noise and interference
  • Lower data rates

Examples: Mobile networks, radio broadcasting

Conclusion

Each transmission medium has its own strengths and weaknesses. Optical fibre offers the highest speed and reliability, copper is cost-effective, wireless provides flexibility, and radio waves enable long-distance communication. The best choice depends on application requirements such as speed, cost, distance, and mobility.
