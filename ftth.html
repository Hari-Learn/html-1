FTTH (Fiber To The Home)

Overview, Architecture, Issues & Testing

What is FTTH?

FTTH (Fiber To The Home) is a broadband network architecture that uses optical fiber to provide high-speed internet directly to residential homes. It delivers faster speeds, lower latency, and better reliability compared to traditional copper-based networks.

FTTH Architecture

  • OLT (Optical Line Terminal): Located at service provider’s central office.
  • ODN (Optical Distribution Network): Fiber cables, splitters, and connectors.
  • Splitter: Divides signal from OLT to multiple users.
  • ONT/ONU: Installed at customer premises to convert optical signals to electrical.

Common FTTH Issues

  • Low optical power levels
  • Fiber cuts or bends
  • Dirty connectors
  • Packet loss and latency issues
  • Slow internet speed
  • ONT device faults

Testing & Troubleshooting

1. Optical Power Measurement

Use a power meter to measure optical signal strength in dBm. Typical range: -8 dBm to -28 dBm.

2. Speed Test

Use speedtest tools to verify bandwidth performance.

3. iPerf Testing

Used to measure throughput between two network devices.

4. Packet Loss Detection

Check for lost packets which indicate network instability.

5. Ping Test

Measure latency and response time to a server.

6. Traceroute

Identify network path and locate delays or failures.

7. Latency Check

Ensure latency is low for real-time applications like gaming or video calls.
