2. Why should a Network Engineer know Active Directory?

In real networks, AD and networking are deeply connected.

  • Most enterprises use AD-based authentication for network access
  • AD relies heavily on DNS, IP, routing, and time sync
  • Network issues often appear as AD login failures
  • Technologies like 802.1X, VPN, NAC, Wi-Fi integrate with AD

3. Network Problems That Are Actually AD Problems

  • Users cannot log in after VPN connection
  • Wi-Fi works but domain login fails
  • Slow logins across sites
  • Group Policy not applying
  • Kerberos authentication errors

üëâ Many of these issues come from DNS misconfiguration, firewall blocks, or latency.

6. Interview Rapid-Fire Answers ‚ö°

Q: Is Active Directory authentication or authorization?
A: Both. AD authenticates users and authorizes access using policies and groups.

Q: Why is DNS critical for AD?
A: AD uses DNS to locate Domain Controllers and services.

Q: Why should a network engineer care about AD?
A: Because most network access depends on AD-based authentication.

Q: What happens if time is out of sync in AD?
A: Kerberos authentication fails.
